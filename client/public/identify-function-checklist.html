Identify Function Checklist

The Identify function is the foundation of NIST CSF 2.0. It answers the question: "What do we need to protect, and what are our priorities?"

📦 Asset Management

"You cannot protect what you don't know you have."

  • Complete inventory of all hardware assets (servers, endpoints, mobile devices) HIGH
  • Complete inventory of all software and applications
  • Inventory of cloud services and SaaS applications
  • Data repositories and databases catalogued
  • IoT and connected devices identified
  • Third-party/vendor access points documented
  • Assets classified by criticality (Critical/High/Medium/Low) HIGH
  • Shadow IT discovery process in place MED
  • Automated asset discovery tools deployed
  • Regular inventory updates scheduled (at least quarterly)

⚠️ Risk Assessment

"Invest resources where the stakes are highest."

  • Threats to each critical asset identified HIGH
  • Vulnerabilities in systems documented
  • Likelihood of threats assessed (High/Medium/Low)
  • Business impact of threats quantified HIGH
  • Risks prioritized based on likelihood × impact
  • Risk register created and maintained
  • Risk appetite defined by leadership MED
  • Risk tolerance thresholds established
  • Regular risk assessments scheduled (at least annually)
  • Risks communicated in business terms to executives HIGH

🏛️ Governance

"Without governance, cybersecurity becomes a game of hot potato."

  • Security policies documented and approved HIGH
  • Roles and responsibilities clearly defined HIGH
  • Executive sponsorship and accountability established
  • Security steering committee or risk council in place MED
  • Incident response roles assigned
  • Third-party vendor security requirements defined
  • Employee security responsibilities communicated
  • Policy review and update schedule established
  • Compliance requirements mapped to controls
  • Security budget approved and aligned with risk priorities

How the Three Pillars Connect

Asset Management tells you what you have.

Risk Assessment tells you what to prioritize.

Governance ensures priorities are executed consistently with accountability.

Without all three, the Identify function is like "a beautifully drawn map with no one to navigate it."

Quick-Start Priority Actions

  • Week 1: Start asset inventory—focus on critical systems first
  • Week 2: Identify top 10 risks based on asset criticality
  • Week 3: Document basic security policies and assign ownership
  • Week 4: Present findings to leadership in business terms
