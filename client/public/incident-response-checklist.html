Incident Response Checklist

When a security incident occurs, speed and organization are critical. Use this checklist to guide your first 60 minutes of response.

Phase 1: Initial Triage 0-15 minutes

  • Confirm the alert is a real incident (not false positive)
  • Assess initial severity: Critical / High / Medium / Low
  • Document the time of detection and initial observations
  • Assign incident commander if not already designated
  • Start incident timeline documentation

Phase 2: Containment 15-30 minutes

  • Isolate affected systems from the network (don't power off)
  • Block malicious IPs/domains at firewall
  • Disable compromised accounts
  • Preserve logs and evidence before they rotate
  • Take memory dumps if possible (for forensics)
  • Document all containment actions taken

Phase 3: Notifications 30-45 minutes

  • Notify incident response team lead / CISO
  • Alert executive sponsor if severity warrants
  • Engage legal counsel (especially for data breaches)
  • Contact HR if insider threat suspected
  • Prepare initial internal communication
  • Determine if external forensics needed

Phase 4: Investigation 45-60 minutes

  • Determine scope: What systems are affected?
  • Identify data at risk: What type? How much?
  • Investigate entry point: How did attackers get in?
  • Check for lateral movement: Are other systems compromised?
  • Establish timeline: When did the attack start?
  • Brief leadership on initial findings

Critical Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't wipe systems immediately - Destroys evidence needed for investigation
  • Don't communicate externally too early - Wait until facts are known
  • Don't assume it's contained - Attackers often have multiple footholds
  • Don't wait until Monday - Friday attacks are intentional; delays cost more
  • Don't go it alone - Engage the full response team

Pre-Establish These Contacts

Don't scramble to find help during an incident. Have these relationships ready:

  • External forensics firm (on retainer if possible)
  • Legal counsel with breach experience
  • Crisis communications support
  • Cyber insurance carrier's incident hotline
  • Law enforcement contacts (FBI, local cyber unit)
