Detect-Respond-Recover Improvement Guide

After an incident, use this guide to identify improvements across all three functions. The goal: detect faster, respond smarter, recover stronger.

The Three Functions Work Together

Detect finds the threat → Respond contains and investigates → Recover restores operations and learns

Weakness in one function cascades to the others. A 4-day detection delay means 4 extra days of damage before response can even begin.

Detect Improvements

Goal: Find threats faster

  • Endpoint Detection (EDR/XDR): Monitors endpoints for suspicious behavior, not just known signatures
  • SIEM with tuned alerts: Aggregate logs and generate meaningful alerts (not noise)
  • 24/7 monitoring: Threats don't keep business hours—consider managed security services
  • Behavioral baselines: Know what "normal" looks like to spot anomalies
  • Threat intelligence: Stay aware of current attack patterns and IOCs
Key Metric: Mean Time to Detect (MTTD) - How long from intrusion to discovery?

Respond Improvements

Goal: Contain and investigate faster

  • Documented IR plan: Written procedures with clear roles and escalation paths
  • Regular tabletop exercises: Practice response before real incidents
  • Pre-established relationships: Forensics, legal, PR on retainer—not scrambling during crisis
  • Network segmentation: Limit blast radius so one compromise doesn't mean full breach
  • Automation: Auto-isolate compromised systems, auto-block known bad IPs
Key Metric: Mean Time to Contain (MTTC) - How long from detection to containment?

Recover Improvements

Goal: Restore operations faster and stronger

  • Tested backups: Regular restoration drills to ensure backups actually work
  • Recovery prioritization: Know which systems to restore first (business critical)
  • Immutable/offline backups: Backups that ransomware can't encrypt or delete
  • Communication plan: Templates ready for customers, regulators, employees
  • Post-incident review: Formal lessons learned process to improve for next time
Key Metric: Mean Time to Recover (MTTR) - How long from containment to normal operations?

Post-Incident Questions

Ask These After Every Incident

  • How long did detection take? What could have caught it faster?
  • Did the response team know their roles? Where was confusion?
  • Did our backups work? How long did restoration take?
  • What controls failed? What controls worked?
  • How can we prevent this specific attack pattern in the future?
  • Did we communicate effectively internally and externally?
