Asset Classification Quick Reference

Use this guide to classify assets by criticality level based on their importance to business operations, sensitivity of data, and regulatory requirements.

Criticality Levels

Critical Crown Jewels

Assets that, if compromised, would cause severe business disruption, major financial loss, or significant regulatory penalties.

  • Customer/patient databases with PII or PHI
  • Payment processing systems (PCI-DSS scope)
  • Core business applications (ERP, CRM)
  • Domain controllers and authentication systems
  • Encryption keys and certificate authorities

High Essential Operations

Assets that support critical business functions or contain sensitive data that could cause significant harm if exposed.

  • Email servers and collaboration platforms
  • HR systems with employee data
  • Source code repositories
  • Backup systems and disaster recovery infrastructure
  • VPN and remote access systems

Medium Business Support

Assets that support business operations but don't directly handle sensitive data or critical functions.

  • Public-facing marketing websites
  • Internal documentation systems
  • Development/testing environments (without real data)
  • General business applications
  • Conference room systems

Low General Assets

Assets with minimal business impact if compromised, though still part of your inventory.

  • IoT devices (smart appliances, environmental sensors)
  • Guest WiFi networks
  • Digital signage systems
  • Non-sensitive printers
Watch Out: Shadow IT

Forgotten or unauthorized systems are often HIGH risk regardless of their original purpose:

  • Test servers that were never decommissioned
  • Applications installed without IT approval
  • Personal devices accessing company resources
  • Cloud services signed up by individual teams

These systems are often unpatched, unmonitored, and perfect entry points for attackers.

Key Questions for Classification
  • What data does this asset store, process, or transmit?
  • What regulations apply to this data (HIPAA, PCI-DSS, GDPR)?
  • What's the business impact if this asset is unavailable for 1 hour? 1 day? 1 week?
  • Could this asset be used as a stepping stone to access more critical systems?
  • Who knows this asset exists, and who is responsible for it?
