Protect Function Quick Guide

The Protect function develops and implements safeguards to ensure delivery of critical services. It's about building multiple layers of defense.

Defense in Depth

No single control is perfect. Layer multiple protections so that if one fails, others still provide security. Think: castle walls, moat, guards, locks, and alarms—not just one.

Protect Categories

🔐 Identity Management & Access Control

Control who gets in and what they can do.

  • Least Privilege: Minimum access needed for the job
  • MFA: Multiple factors to prove identity
  • Account Lifecycle: Provision, review, deprovision
  • Zero Trust: Never trust, always verify

💾 Data Security

Protect data in all its states.

  • At Rest: Encrypt stored data
  • In Transit: Use TLS/HTTPS for transfers
  • In Use: Access controls and monitoring
  • Classification: Not all data needs same protection
  • DLP: Prevent unauthorized data exfiltration

🎓 Awareness & Training

Your people are your first line of defense.

  • Engaging: Real scenarios, not boring videos
  • Frequent: Monthly micro-training, not annual marathons
  • Measured: Track phishing click rates, report rates
  • Culture: Security is everyone's responsibility

🛡️ Protective Technology

Tools that defend your environment.

  • Firewalls: Control network traffic
  • EDR/XDR: Endpoint detection and response
  • IDS/IPS: Detect and prevent intrusions
  • Segmentation: Limit blast radius of breaches
  • AI/Automation: Speed and scale for modern threats

Common Pitfall: Tool Overload

Buying more tools doesn't mean better security. Organizations often end up with overlapping products, alert fatigue, and integration gaps. Focus on a cohesive, well-orchestrated defense rather than a pile of fancy gadgets.

Industry Context Matters

Different industries have different protection priorities:

  • Healthcare: PHI protection, HIPAA compliance
  • Financial: PCI-DSS, fraud prevention
  • Legal: Client confidentiality, privilege protection
  • Retail: Payment security, customer data
