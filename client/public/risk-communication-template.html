Executive Risk Communication Template

Use this template to translate technical security risks into business language that executives can understand and act upon.

The Executive Summary Template

Structure Your Message

  1. What's happening? [Describe the issue in plain language]
  2. Why is it urgent? [Explain the threat level and timeline]
  3. What's the business impact? [Quantify in dollars, customers, or reputation]
  4. What do we need to do? [Clear action item with timeline]
  5. What happens if we don't act? [Consequences of inaction]

Technical to Business Translation

Avoid This Jargon

Technical Term Business Translation
CVE / Vulnerability Security flaw / weakness in our systems
RCE (Remote Code Execution) Attackers could take control of our systems
CVSS Score 9.8 Extremely serious / critical severity
Unpatched servers Systems missing security updates
Exploit code available Attack tools are publicly available
Data exfiltration Theft of customer/company data
Lateral movement Attackers spreading through our network

Before and After Examples

Too Technical

"CVE-2024-1234 is a critical RCE vulnerability in Apache Struts 2.5.30 with a CVSS score of 9.8. We have 3 unpatched production servers and exploit code is publicly available on GitHub."

Executive-Friendly

"We've found a serious security flaw in three of our customer-facing systems. Hackers are actively exploiting this at other companies, and attack tools are freely available. We need to apply updates within 48 hours to protect customer data."

Quantifying Business Impact

Make It Real with Numbers
  • Revenue at risk: "This system processes $X million in transactions annually"
  • Customer impact: "X customers' data could be exposed"
  • Regulatory fines: "HIPAA violations can cost up to $X per record"
  • Downtime cost: "Each hour of outage costs us $X in lost sales"
  • Recovery costs: "Average breach costs $X million to remediate"

Example: Complete Executive Summary

"We've discovered a critical security vulnerability in three of our customer-facing web servers. Hackers are actively exploiting this flaw at other companies, and attack tools are freely available online.

If exploited, attackers could take complete control of these servers, potentially accessing our customer database with 500,000 records and disrupting our online services.

We need to apply security patches within the next 48 hours, which will require a 2-hour maintenance window on Saturday night.

Delaying this fix significantly increases our risk of a breach that could cost us $2-5 million in recovery costs, regulatory fines, and reputation damage."
