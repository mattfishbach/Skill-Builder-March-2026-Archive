Review / Refine / Repeat Guide

Building strong relationships is a continuous development process. This simple three-step cycle helps you systematically improve how you interact with others.

1. Review Gather feedback
→
2. Refine Make adjustments
→
3. Repeat Keep improving

Step 1: Review

Understand how others perceive you by gathering feedback. Choose a few trusted colleagues or friends and ask for their honest perspective.

"How do you think I generally come across to others?"

"How would you describe me when I'm in a team environment?"

"Is there anything specific I'm good at when it comes to interacting with other people?"

"What could I develop when it comes to maintaining relationships?"

Key mindset: Listen to feedback without getting self-critical or defensive. Feedback is an opinion, not a fact. Gather multiple perspectives for a rounded view.

Step 2: Refine

Make small adjustments based on the feedback. One small step at a time. Over time, these small tweaks compound to create big results.

Focus on what seems most impactful and most achievable. Don't try to change everything at once.

Step 3: Repeat

We're all a work in progress. Repeat the cycle continuously. Taking the emotion out of feedback and turning it into a process makes it much more manageable.

Common relationship management strengths include:

Getting your message across effectively
Active Listening

Making Others Comfortable
Helping people feel understood
Reading Group Dynamics
Quickly picking up on team energy
Authentic Connection
Building genuine, trusted relationships
Adaptability
Adjusting your style to different people

Intent vs. Impact

Key insight: How someone perceives your message can be more important than the message itself.

Rather than focusing on the exact words you want to say, focus on: What do I want the other person to take away from this interaction?

Benefits of focusing on intent:

  • Boosts confidence — You understand it doesn't matter exactly how you deliver the message; people will connect with the intent behind it
  • Allows flexibility — You can adapt your communication style to different people and situations
  • Creates authenticity — You'll come across as natural and genuine, which builds trust

Reflection Questions

  • What are your natural strengths when interacting with others?
  • How do others perceive you? (If unsure, this is a great opportunity to ask!)
  • Do you listen intently to others and really hear what they're saying?
  • When communicating, do you focus more on what you want to say or what you want the other person to take away?
  • Are there relationships where you could apply the Review/Refine/Repeat cycle right now?
Remember: Effective communication is about the message received by the other person, not the message you want to give. Match your tone, language, and body language to help communicate your intent.
